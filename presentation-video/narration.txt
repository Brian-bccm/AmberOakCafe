Hello, and welcome to the Amber and Oak Cafe website presentation. This is a professional restaurant landing website built for a premium cafe, restaurant, kopitiam, or small food business. The goal is to help customers understand the brand, browse the menu, view the atmosphere, find the location, and contact the business quickly.

The live website is published on Netlify, and the source code is available on GitHub. The homepage starts with a strong hero section. It shows the cafe name, a short value message, and two main actions: view the menu, or order and reserve through WhatsApp.

The navigation links move smoothly to each section: About, Menu, Gallery, Special Offer, Location, and Contact. On mobile, the menu changes into a compact button so the layout stays clean and easy to use.

The About section explains the cafe concept and creates trust. The Menu section shows realistic food items and prices, including signature brunch plates, local fusion items, coffee, lunch bowls, and dessert. The Gallery section shows food, drinks, and cafe ambience. This is important because restaurant customers often decide quickly based on visuals.

The Special Offer section promotes a Weekend Brunch Set. This area can be reused for seasonal promos, festive menus, lunch deals, or opening offers.

The Location section gives the address, opening hours, parking and takeaway notes, and a Google Maps link. The floating WhatsApp button stays visible while customers scroll. This helps turn visitors into reservations or orders without forcing them to search for contact details.

The contact form is connected through Netlify Forms. It validates required fields, shows a helpful message if something is missing, and confirms when the enquiry is ready.

The website is designed to work well on phone and desktop. On mobile, the hero text becomes easier to read, buttons stack vertically, the stats stay compact, and the navigation becomes a mobile menu. This matters because many restaurant customers will open the site from WhatsApp, Instagram, Google Maps, or QR codes.

The project was tested with an automated test suite. The tests check the required sections, menu content, WhatsApp link, and contact form behavior. The lint check confirms the code style is clean. The production build confirms the website can compile successfully for deployment. The project is committed and pushed to GitHub, and Netlify reports the production deployment as ready.

For a real restaurant client, the content can be changed quickly. We can update the restaurant name, menu items, prices, images, address, opening hours, WhatsApp number, email, promotions, and brand colors.

This project is a strong freelance portfolio example because it solves real small business needs: professional branding, mobile-first layout, clear menu presentation, direct WhatsApp contact, contact form enquiries, and a live deployed website.
